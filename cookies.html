1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, smartphone, tablet) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, enhance user experience, and provide information to the site owners. Cookies are not viruses or malware; they are simple data files that can be read by the website that set them.

There are different types of cookies:

  • Session cookies: Temporary cookies that are erased when you close your browser.
  • Persistent cookies: Remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them manually.
  • First-party cookies: Set by the website you are visiting (e.g., thefloollab.com).
  • Third-party cookies: Set by domains other than the one you are visiting (e.g., analytics or advertising partners).

3. How Long Do Cookies Last?

The lifespan of a cookie varies depending on its type and purpose. Session cookies are deleted when you close your browser. Persistent cookies have expiration dates set by the website – these can range from a few hours to several years. We generally set our persistent cookies to expire within 30 days to 2 years, depending on the function. You can always delete cookies manually via your browser settings at any time.
