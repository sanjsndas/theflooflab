Privacy Policy

your data, your floof, your trust

Last updated: June 28, 2026

At The Floof Lab (“we,” “our,” “us”), we take your privacy seriously. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website, book our services, or interact with us online or in person. Please read this policy carefully. If you do not agree with the terms, please do not access the site or use our services.

1. Information We Collect

We collect several types of information from and about users of our website and services, including:

  • Personal Data: When you book a grooming appointment, create an account, or contact us, we may collect your name, email address, postal address, phone number, payment details (processed securely via third-party processors), and information about your puppy (name, breed, age, health conditions, and grooming preferences).
  • Usage Data: We automatically collect information about your device and how you interact with our site, such as IP address, browser type, pages visited, time and date of visit, referral URLs, and other diagnostic data. This helps us improve user experience and site performance.
  • Cookies and Tracking: We use cookies and similar technologies to remember your preferences, analyse trends, and personalise content. You can control cookie settings in your browser; however, disabling cookies may affect some site features. For more details, see our Cookie Policy.
  • Photos and Media: With your consent, we may take photos of your puppy during grooming sessions for our portfolio, social media, or promotional materials. You may withdraw consent at any time.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for various purposes, including:

  • To provide, maintain, and improve our grooming services and customer support.
  • To process transactions and send you booking confirmations, reminders, and updates.
  • To personalise your experience and tailor content, offers, and recommendations.
  • To communicate with you about promotions, events, and new services (you may opt out at any time).
  • To monitor and analyse usage trends and site performance.
  • To detect, prevent, and address technical issues, fraud, or security risks.
  • To comply with legal obligations and enforce our terms and policies.
Our promise: We never sell your personal data to third parties. We only share it with trusted service providers (e.g., payment processors, email platforms) who adhere to strict data protection standards.

3. Sharing and Disclosure

We may share your information in the following limited circumstances:

  • Service Providers: We engage third-party vendors to perform functions such as payment processing, email delivery, analytics, and customer relationship management. They have access to your data only as needed to perform these functions and are contractually obligated to maintain confidentiality.
  • Legal Requirements: We may disclose your information if required by law, court order, or government regulation, or if we believe that such disclosure is necessary to protect our rights, property, or safety, or that of our clients or the public.
  • Business Transfers: In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, your information may be transferred to the new owner, who will continue to be bound by this Privacy Policy.
  • With Your Consent: We may share your information for other purposes with your explicit consent.

4. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal information against accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, or unauthorised disclosure. These include:

  • Encryption of data in transit (SSL/TLS) and at rest where feasible.
  • Access controls and authentication protocols for internal systems.
  • Regular security assessments and employee training on data protection.
  • Secure storage of physical records (e.g., paper forms) in locked cabinets.

Despite our efforts, no method of transmission over the Internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. While we strive to protect your data, we cannot guarantee absolute security. In the event of a data breach, we will notify affected users and relevant authorities as required by law.

5. Data Retention

We retain your personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes outlined in this policy, unless a longer retention period is required or permitted by law. For example, we keep appointment records for up to 7 years for insurance and legal compliance, and we may retain anonymised data for analytical purposes indefinitely. You may request deletion of your data at any time, subject to legal obligations.

6. Your Rights and Choices

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have certain rights regarding your personal data, including:

  • Access: Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Rectification: Correct inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Erasure: Request deletion of your data (subject to legal retention requirements).
  • Restriction: Limit how we use your data in certain circumstances.
  • Portability: Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Objection: Object to processing for direct marketing or legitimate interests.

To exercise these rights, please contact us at privacy@thefloollab.com or call +1 (800) 555-FLOOF. We will respond within 30 days. We may ask for verification of identity to protect your privacy.

7. Children’s Privacy

Our services are not directed to children under the age of 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you are a parent or guardian and you believe your child has provided us with personal information, please contact us immediately. We will take steps to delete such information.

8. International Transfers

We are based in the United States, and your information may be transferred to, stored, or processed in countries other than your own. These countries may have different data protection laws. However, we take steps to ensure that your data is treated securely and in accordance with this policy, including using standard contractual clauses where applicable.

9. Updates to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or legal requirements. We will notify you of material changes by posting the new policy on this page with a revised “Last updated” date. We encourage you to review this page periodically. Your continued use of our services after any changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

10. Contact Us

If you have any questions, concerns, or complaints about this Privacy Policy or our data handling practices, please reach out to our Data Protection Officer:

  • Email: privacy@thefloollab.com

11. Detailed Data Processing Activities

We collect data through various touchpoints: our website forms, in-store sign-in tablets, phone calls, and direct emails. For each grooming session, we record your puppy's weight, coat condition, behaviour notes, and any special instructions you provide. This information is stored in our secure CRM system, accessible only to trained staff. We use this history to tailor future sessions, track health trends, and send birthday or seasonal reminders. We also maintain a “Floof Diary” for each puppy, where we note favourite treats, calming techniques, and even the songs that soothe them. This enhances your puppy’s experience and builds a lasting bond.

We also analyse aggregated, anonymised data to understand popular services, peak times, and client demographics. This helps us optimise staffing, improve service offerings, and plan community events. We never use this data to make automated decisions that significantly affect you; all booking confirmations and adjustments are handled by our caring team.

12. Photography and Marketing

With your explicit consent, we capture high-quality images and short videos of your puppy during grooming. These may be used on our website, social media (Instagram, Facebook, TikTok), and promotional materials. We never use images that depict stress or discomfort. You can withdraw consent at any time by emailing us, and we will remove existing media from public channels as soon as possible. We also offer a “no-photo” option during booking if you prefer privacy.

13. Payment Information

We do not store your credit card or bank details on our own servers. All payments are processed through reputable third-party payment gateways (e.g., Stripe, Square) that are PCI-DSS compliant. We only receive confirmation of payment and the last four digits of your card for reference. For in-store payments, we use encrypted point-of-sale terminals. We never record CVV numbers or full PANs.

14. Third-Party Links and Services

Our website may contain links to external sites (e.g., our booking platform, social media, or partner organisations). We are not responsible for the privacy practices or content of these third parties. We encourage you to review their privacy policies before interacting with them.

15. Legal Basis for Processing (GDPR/CCPA)

For users in the European Economic Area or California, we process your data on the following legal bases: (a) performance of a contract (e.g., to provide grooming services), (b) compliance with legal obligations, (c) our legitimate interests (e.g., improving services, preventing fraud), and (d) your consent (e.g., for marketing emails). You have the right to withdraw consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing based on consent before its withdrawal. Under the CCPA, California residents have the right to request disclosure of categories and specific pieces of personal data collected, as well as the right to opt out of the sale of personal data (which we do not conduct). To exercise your CCPA rights, please contact us using the details above.

16. Data Breach Procedures

In the unlikely event of a data breach, we have established incident response procedures to contain, investigate, and mitigate the impact. We will notify affected individuals and regulatory authorities within 72 hours of becoming aware, where legally required. Our team receives regular training on breach detection and reporting, and we conduct periodic drills to ensure readiness.

17. Commitment to Transparency

We believe that transparency builds trust. That's why we've dedicated this entire page to explaining our practices in clear, non-legalistic language. We welcome feedback on how we can improve our privacy communications. You can also request a copy of our Data Processing Agreement (DPA) if you are a corporate client or partner. We are proud to be a privacy-first business, and we regularly audit our practices against industry standards like ISO 27701 and the NIST Privacy Framework.

Floof’s promise: We will never spam you, sell your data, or use your puppy's photos without enthusiastic consent. Your privacy is as important to us as your puppy's happiness.

We hope this policy gives you confidence in our commitment to protecting your data. If you have any further questions, don't hesitate to reach out – we're here to help. Thank you for being part of The Floof Lab family.
